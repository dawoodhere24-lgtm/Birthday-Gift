her story didn't start when i arrived. it started years earlier, in chapters i never got to witness. chapters that quietly built the person i can't imagine living without.

— the school years —

before she knew the world was watching

look at her. small and certain, that huge smile filling the whole frame. she already had that thing, that warmth, that completely unguarded joy, even then. before she had any idea what the world would ask of her, she showed up like she already belonged.

— the very beginning —

the girl with the red bag

there is something about this photo that feels like looking at a secret. a version of her that existed before i had the privilege of knowing her. looking sideways at something, already curious, already someone worth knowing.

— growing up —

all white, all her

she shaded her eyes from the sun like she was already looking for something. i like to think it was the future. i like to think, in some version of the universe, she was already looking toward where we'd end up.

— becoming —

the balaclava era

she put a filter over her face and thought she was hiding. she wasn't. those eyes gave everything away. they always do. no filter in the world is going to make her less readable to someone who has been paying attention.

“every year she lived before i knew her was quietly writing the person i would completely fall for.”

01

she makes people feel less alone

there is something specific about being around her. you don't have to explain yourself as much. you don't have to earn your place in the conversation. she just makes room, without fanfare, without condition, and most people don't realise how rare that is until it's gone.

02

she changes the temperature of a room

i have watched it happen. she arrives and something loosens. people laugh easier. conversations go deeper. it is not something she performs, it is just what she carries. i don't know if she knows she does it. she should.

03

she holds the things you say

she listens like she's archiving. like she genuinely intends to carry what you've said. weeks later she'll come back to it unprompted, checking in, remembering something mentioned in passing. that kind of love is a love language most people can't even name.

04

she made me want to be better

not because she asked me to. not because she said anything. just because knowing her set a standard, for gentleness, for honesty, for trying. she became the version of a person i wanted to be close enough to. that is the quietest and most powerful thing one person can do to another.

05

she carries more than she shows

i have watched her deal with hard things without asking anyone to carry them with her. quietly. with more grace than the situation deserved. and still showed up soft for the people around her. that is not nothing. that is one of the bravest things i have ever seen.

“she has, without asking for credit, without announcement, consistently made the world around her warmer.”
04

things i hope
never change

the small things. the specific things. the things that are so completely her that losing them would feel like losing something essential.

🌙

the way she laughs at her own jokes

she starts laughing before she finishes the punchline. every time. she cannot help it. and it is the most genuinely infectious thing, the laugh is always better than the joke, and the joke is usually good.

✨

those eyes when she's hiding something

she thinks she's being subtle. she is not. there is a very specific look she gets when she's deciding whether to say the thing or swallow it, and i have memorised every version of it.

🌸

the way she loves

she loves in small, consistent ways that add up to something enormous. she doesn't announce it. she just does it. over and over. that kind of love doesn't shout. it builds.

💫

her stubbornness

she will hold a position until someone gives her an actual reason to change it. not pressure. not guilt. a reason. i used to find it maddening. now i think it is one of the most principled things about her.

🦋

the overthinking she calls a flaw

she feels everything so thoroughly. turns it over. examines it. cares about getting it right. she calls that a problem. i call it proof that she takes the world seriously. please never stop taking the world seriously.

⭐

the balaclava thing

she covered her face with a filter and still managed to be the most recognisable person in the photo. those eyes. that pout. no amount of knitted fabric was ever going to be enough. i don't know why she tries.

“i hope she stays exactly this unfinished, this real, this completely and entirely herself.”

happy birthday ✦ 2026

faryal dawood

i don't have the right words. i never do when it comes to you. but i'm going to try anyway, because someone as special as you deserves more than a quick "happy birthday." you deserve to know how much light you bring into the lives around you, and how much brighter mine became the moment you walked into it.

one thing i've never forgotten is the way you get excited when you're talking about something you genuinely care about. the way your messages suddenly get longer, the way you keep going even when you think you're talking too much, and the way i could sit there for hours just reading every word. i don't think you realize how beautiful that is. i don't think you realize how beautiful you are in those moments, when you're completely yourself and not trying to impress anyone.

you've become such a huge part of my life that it's hard to remember what things felt like before you. somehow you've managed to make ordinary days feel less ordinary. you've been there through my good moods, my bad moods, my random thoughts, my stupid jokes, and all the parts of me that aren't always easy to deal with. and through all of it, you've given me memories, comfort, laughter, and happiness that i genuinely wouldn't trade for anything.

more than anything, i hope you find peace with yourself. i hope one day you see even a fraction of what everyone else sees when they look at you. i hope you stop being so hard on yourself. i hope you chase the things that make you happy without doubting whether you're good enough, because you are. i hope you wake up every morning knowing that you deserve kindness, love, success, and every good thing that comes your way. not because you've earned it by being perfect, but because you're already enough exactly as you are.

and for the record, you do not need to lose weight, change yourself, or become somebody else to be beautiful. you already are. i've never looked at you and thought about what should be different. i've only ever looked at you and thought about how lucky i am that you exist.

"thank you for being born. because if you weren't, i never would have met the person who quietly, permanently rearranged everything."
